Garlic Bread Recipe

Ingredients
  • ½ cup/8 tablespoons unsalted butter (1 stick), melted
  • 4 fat garlic cloves, grated
  • ½ cup Parmesan, grated
  • ¼ cup firmly packed chopped flat-leaf parsley
  • ¼ teaspoon kosher salt
  • Black pepper
  • 1 baguette or rustic crusty loaf
  • ½ pound fresh mozzarella, thinly sliced (optional)
Directions
  1. Heat the oven to 400 degrees.
  2. In a small bowl, stir together the butter,
    garlic, Parmesan and parsley.
  3. Add the salt and generously season with pepper.
  4. Set aside while you cut the baguette.
  5. Cut deep slits into the baguette, 1-inch apart
    — don’t cut all the way through the loaf — and
    place the baguette on a large piece of foil.
  6. Using a teaspoon or an offset spatula, generously
    spread the seasoned butter inside each slit.
  7. Make sure to reach the bottom of the slit; don’t
    be shy here.
  8. Wrap the baguette in the foil, place on a baking
    sheet and bake for 15 minutes.
  9. Remove the baguette from the oven and unwrap the
    top (leave the bread on the foil).
  10. If you’re adding mozzarella, use the offset
    spatula or spoon to gently open each slit and tuck a
    piece of mozzarella in (it’s O.K. if the cheese pops out
    from the top a little
  11. Return bread to the oven and bake until crisp and
    golden on top, another 5 minutes.
  12. Slice with a serrated knife (or tear with your hands)
    and serve at once.
